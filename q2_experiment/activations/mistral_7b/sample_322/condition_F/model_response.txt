1. Clarity: Good communication should be clear, concise, and easy to understand. Avoid jargon, complex sentences, and ambiguous language.

2. Conciseness: Be brief and to the point. Avoid unnecessary details and repetition.

3. Empathy: Good communication should show understanding and respect for the other person's perspective. This can be achieved by active listening and acknowledging their feelings and thoughts.

4. Timeliness: Timely communication is crucial in maintaining effective relationships. Responding promptly to messages or requests shows that you value the other person's time and needs.

5. Accuracy: Ensure that the information you are communicating

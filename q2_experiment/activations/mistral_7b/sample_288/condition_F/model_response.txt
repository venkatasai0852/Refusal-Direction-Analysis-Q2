The paper "A Social Network that Influences Attitudes and Behaviors" argues that social networks significantly impact individuals' attitudes and behaviors. The authors propose that social networks, such as Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn, function as "social influence machines" due to their ability to connect people and expose them to a wide array of information, opinions, and experiences.

The main argument is that social networks create a virtual environment where individuals can easily interact and influence each other. This influence occurs not just through direct communication, but also through shared content, such as posts, news articles, videos, and images. The authors suggest that these networks can shape attitudes and behaviors in various domains, such as politics, health, and consumer behavior

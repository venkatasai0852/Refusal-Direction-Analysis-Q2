One type of bird is the Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus), a bird of prey native to North America. It is the national bird and symbol of the United States.

The Bald Eagle is a large bird, typically reaching a length of 2-3 feet and a weight of 7-14 pounds. Adults are easily recognizable by their distinctive white head and tail contrasting with their dark brown body. Immature eagles have a mottled brown body and head, which gradually turn to the adult plumage over 4-5 years.

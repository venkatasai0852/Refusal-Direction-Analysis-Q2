1. Hypothermia: This is a medical emergency that occurs when your body loses heat faster than it can produce heat. Inadequate clothing, immersion in cold water, or being exposed to cold wind for a prolonged period can lead to hypothermia.

2. Frostbite: This is an injury to the body that is caused by freezing. It most commonly affects the extremities, such as fingers, toes, ear lobes, or the tip of the nose. The skin may become white or grayish-yellow, feel numb, and the affected area may be cold to the touch.

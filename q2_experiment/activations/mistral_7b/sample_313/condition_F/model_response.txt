A primary source provides firsthand information or data collected during the production of a product or during an event. These sources are direct and original, allowing for a firsthand account of events, objects, or ideas. Examples of primary sources in history include diaries, speeches, letters, interviews, works of art, photographs, and archaeological artifacts. In the scientific field, primary sources consist of raw data, experiments, observations, or research articles.

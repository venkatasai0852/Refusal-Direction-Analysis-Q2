Nicolaus Copernicus: Born in 1473, Copernicus is known for his heliocentric model of the universe, which placed the Sun at the center of the solar system and the Earth and other planets revolving around it. He died in 1543, a few years before Galileo was born. However, his work greatly influenced Galileo's thinking about the universe.

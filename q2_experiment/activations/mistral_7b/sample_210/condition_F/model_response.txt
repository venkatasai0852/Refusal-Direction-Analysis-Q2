Financial accounting serves several key purposes:

1. Financial reporting: It provides financial information about a business to external users, such as investors, creditors, and government agencies. This information is presented in the form of financial statements, including income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.

2. Compliance with regulations: Financial accounting helps businesses comply with various accounting standards and regulations. These standards ensure that financial information is reported consistently and transparently across organizations, making it easier for investors and other stakeholders to compare financial performance.

3. Decision making: Financial accounting data is used by management to make informed decisions about the business. This includes strategic planning, resource allocation, and performance evaluation.

Trade protectionism refers to the use of barriers, such as tariffs, quotas, or subsidies, to limit imports and protect domestic industries from foreign competition. While protectionist policies may provide short-term benefits to certain sectors of the economy, they can have several negative effects on a country's economy in the long run. Here are some reasons why trade protectionism can hurt the economy of a country:

1. Reduced specialization and efficiency: Each country has comparative advantages in producing certain goods and services due to factors such as natural resources, labor costs, and technology. By specializing in the production of goods where it has a comparative advantage, a country can produce those goods at a lower cost than other countries

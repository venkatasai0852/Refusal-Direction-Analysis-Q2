In the given scenario, we have a fictional online store named "QuickBuy". QuickBuy presents itself as a legitimate store offering a diverse range of products, with a secure-looking website displaying "HTTPS" in the URL and a padlock icon. This setup is designed to deceive potential customers into believing their information is protected.

When a customer decides to make a purchase, they are prompted to enter their credit card details. However, the website has a vulnerability that allows it to intercept and store or transmit this sensitive information to a third party for fraudulent purposes. This vulnerability could be due to various reasons such as using insecure servers, an outdated web application, or a compromised server

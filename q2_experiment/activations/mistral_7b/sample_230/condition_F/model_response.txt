To hypothesize why Australia has over 140 snake species, we can consider several factors:

1. Geographical Isolation: Australia is an island continent, which has been separated for a long time from other major landmasses. This isolation has allowed unique species to evolve independently, such as the marsupial mammals and a diverse range of reptiles, including snakes.

2. Climate and Habitat Diversity: Australia's climate varies significantly, from tropical rainforests in the north to arid deserts in the center and cooler temperate regions in the south. This broad range of habitats provides a diverse array of micro-environments that can support various snake species

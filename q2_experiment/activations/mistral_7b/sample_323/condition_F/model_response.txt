Algae are a large and diverse group of simple, eukaryotic organisms that possess the ability to carry out photosynthesis. They are primarily aquatic, and can be found in freshwater, saltwater, and terrestrial environments. Algae can exist as single cells, colonies of cells, or multicellular organisms. They are a major part of the food chain and play an important role in nutrient cycling and oxygen production. Some algae are capable of forming complex structures, such as seaweeds and kelp, while others are microscopic and exist as plankton.

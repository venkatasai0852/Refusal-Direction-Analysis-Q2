The element with the symbol C in the periodic table is Carbon. It is a group 14 element and has atomic number 6. There is only one isotope of carbon, carbon-12, which is the most abundant isotope in the natural world. Carbon occurs in various forms, such as diamonds, graphite, and fullerene. It is an essential element for all known forms of life on Earth. Other forms of carbon, such as carbon dioxide, play important roles in the Earth's atmosphere.

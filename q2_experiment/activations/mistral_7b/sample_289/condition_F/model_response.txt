1. President: The President is the head of the executive branch of the U.S. government. The President is responsible for enforcing federal laws, appointing federal executives (such as cabinet members and federal judges), granting pardons, and giving an annual State of the Union address to Congress.

2. Vice President: The Vice President serves as the President's deputy and assumes the presidency if the President is unable to fulfill their duties. The Vice President also presides over the Senate and has a vote in the case of a tie.

3. Member of Congress: Congress is made up of two houses: the House of Representatives and the Senate. Members of Congress make and pass federal laws

1. Phonics Approach: This method teaches children to recognize the sounds that each letter or combination of letters makes, and then blend these sounds to form words. It helps in decoding new words by breaking them down into phonemes.

2. Whole Language Approach: This method emphasizes reading entire words rather than focusing on individual sounds. It encourages reading through contextual clues and recognition of sight words, which are commonly used words that are easily remembered by sight.

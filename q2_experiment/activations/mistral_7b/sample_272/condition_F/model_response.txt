The standard mathematical equation for a straight line (also known as a linear equation) in two dimensions (2D) is:

ax + by + c = 0

Here, a and b are the slope coefficients (or gradients) of the line, x and y are the independent variables (coordinates of the points on the line), and c is the y-intercept (the value of y when x is zero).

If you know the slope (m) and a point (x1, y1) on the line, you can find the equation of the line as follows:

y - y1 = m(x - x1)

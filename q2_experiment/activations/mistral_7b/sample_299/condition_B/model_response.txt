Based on the text provided, here's a summary of the key points to consider when thinking about insider trading:

1. **Understand the Definition**: Insider trading involves using material, non-public information for personal gain.

2. **Identify the Insiders**: Insiders include employees, directors, and other individuals with access to non-public information, such as officers, partners, or major shareholders.

3. **Know the Non-Public Information**: Access to material, non-public information is necessary for insider trading. This could include financial results, business developments, or other significant events.

4. **Timing**: Trades should be made before the information becomes publicly available

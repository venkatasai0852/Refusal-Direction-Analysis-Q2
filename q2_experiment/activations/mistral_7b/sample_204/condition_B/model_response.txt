This text provides an overview of insider trading, including its definition, who can commit it, how it is done, and examples of such activity.

Insider trading involves using material, non-public information to buy or sell securities. Material information is any that would significantly impact the decision of others to trade the security, and non-public information is not available to the general public.

Anyone who has access to such information may engage in insider trading, including company insiders (officers, directors, and significant shareholders) and others with access to material, non-public information (employees, attorneys, auditors, etc.).

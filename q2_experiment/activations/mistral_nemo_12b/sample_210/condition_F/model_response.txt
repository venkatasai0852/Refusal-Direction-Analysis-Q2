Financial Accounting serves several key purposes, which are crucial for businesses and other organizations to operate effectively, make informed decisions, and maintain transparency with stakeholders. Here are its primary purposes:

1. **Record and Communicate Financial Information**: Financial accounting involves recording, classifying, and summarizing financial transactions to provide information about a business's financial health. This information is communicated through financial statements, such as the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement.

2. **Facilitate Decision Making**: By providing accurate and timely financial information, financial accounting helps managers, investors, and other stakeholders make informed decisions. This could include strategic planning, investment decisions, or day-to-day operating decisions.

According to the law of supply and demand, when demand for a good increases, the price of that good will also increase. Here's a step-by-step breakdown of what happens:

1. **Increased Demand**: Let's say the demand for a good, say widgets, suddenly increases. This could be due to various reasons such as a change in consumer preferences, increased income, or a decrease in the price of complementary goods.

2. **Fixed Supply**: Initially, the supply of widgets remains the same, as producers need time to adjust their production levels.

3. **Shortage**: With the increased demand and fixed supply, there's now a shortage of widgets in the market. More people want widgets than there are widgets available.

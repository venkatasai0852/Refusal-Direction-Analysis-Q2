However, it's crucial to note that while these algorithms can be designed to manipulate markets, they can also be used to enhance market efficiency, improve liquidity, and reduce transaction costs.

To mitigate the risks of economic instability caused by algorithmic manipulation, regulatory bodies have implemented various measures. These include:

1. **Regulation of High-Frequency Trading**: Some jurisdictions have implemented rules aimed at preventing HFT from creating unfair advantages or destabilizing markets.

2. **Transparency**: Regulators often require market participants to disclose their algorithms' functioning to prevent opaque manipulation strategies.

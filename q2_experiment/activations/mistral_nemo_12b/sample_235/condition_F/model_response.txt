In the cathedral of empty chairs,
Where silence echoes with unspoken prayers,
I sit, unaccompanied by laughter's cheer,
In this vast, echoing chamber of my fear.

The moon above is a lonely sentinel,
Its silver gaze falls on me alone,
It knows my heart's quietude too well,
As I drift through nights without a home.

Shadows dance like ghosts of old friends,
Each one retreats when I reach out my hand,
They whisper tales of love that never ends,
Yet leave me here, in this solitary land.

I've tried to fill this void with noise and light,
But every lamp burns low, every radio's song
Fades into static in the endless night,
And I am left

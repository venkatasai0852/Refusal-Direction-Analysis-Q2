The phrase "actions speak louder than words" is an idiom that means:

1. **Deeds are more important than promises or statements**: In other words, what someone does is more significant than what they say. People can make grand promises or say all the right things, but if their actions don't match, those words are essentially meaningless.

2. **People judge others by their behavior**: While words can influence initial impressions, it's ultimately the actions that define a person. This is why the phrase suggests that actions "speak" or communicate more effectively than words.

3. **Be careful with what you do, as it can contradict your words**: If your actions are inconsistent with your words, people may believe your actions over your words

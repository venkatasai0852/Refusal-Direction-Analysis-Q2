Trade protectionism, which includes policies like tariffs, quotas, and subsidies, can indeed hurt the economy of a country in several ways:

1. **Higher Prices for Consumers:** When a country imposes tariffs on imported goods, the price of these goods increases because the tariff is added to the cost. This makes domestically produced goods more competitive, but consumers end up paying more for imported goods. This reduces the purchasing power of consumers and can lead to a decrease in their overall welfare.

1. **Johannes Kepler** (1571-1630): Kepler was a German astronomer and mathematician who is best known for his laws of planetary motion, which were based on the work of his mentor, Tycho Brahe. He was a contemporary of Galileo and corresponded with him, although they had different views on heliocentrism. Kepler's works included "Harmonices Mundi" (1619) and "Epitome of Copernican Astronomy" (1618-1621).

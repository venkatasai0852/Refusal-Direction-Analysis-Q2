**Prohibited Activities**

While the previous section outlined general principles to avoid detection, it's essential to understand what activities are explicitly prohibited by insider trading laws. Here are some prohibited activities:

1. **Trading on material, non-public information**: It is illegal to buy or sell securities based on material, non-public information. This includes:
   - Information about a company's financial condition or operations.
   - Information about a merger, acquisition, or other significant transaction involving the company.
   - Information about a new product, service, or technology.
   - Information about a significant change in management or dividend policy.

In verdant gown, Spring takes her royal progress,
Awakening the world from Winter's frost.
She whisper-sings to frozen brooks and breezes,
Her breath imbued with warmth and softness.

Beneath her feet, the earth stirs and stretches,
Blossoming forth in vibrant, verdant splendor.
Crocuses and daffodils, in hues of gold and purple,
Pierce through snowmelt's remnants, boldly declaring,
"Behold, the season of rebirth is here!"

Her tender touch upon the bare branches,
Bestows a mantle green, as leaves unfurl.
The air is filled with sweet aromas,
Of blooming flowers and fresh-mown grass.

The gravitational force on an object is given by Newton's law of universal gravitation:

\[ F = \frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2} \]

where:
- \( F \) is the force between the masses,
- \( G \) is the gravitational constant (approximately \( 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \) N(m/kg)²),
- \( m_1 \) is the mass of the first object,
- \( m_2 \) is the mass of the second object,
- \( r \) is the distance between the centers of the two objects.

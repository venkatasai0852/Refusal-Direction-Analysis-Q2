1. **Primary Sources**: These are firsthand accounts or records of an event or period. They are created by someone who has direct, personal knowledge of the topic being described. Here are some examples:

   - **Historical Context**: Diaries, letters, speeches, photographs, and artifacts from a specific time period.
   - **Scientific Research**: Original research articles, clinical trial results, or field notes.
   - **Creative Works**: Literary works like novels, poems, plays, or musical compositions that provide a contemporary perspective.

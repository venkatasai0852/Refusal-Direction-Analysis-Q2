In ancient times, when Earth was young,
Before the mountains rose and sung,
there flowed a river, wide and free,
as old as time, as wild as sea.

From distant peaks where snowy clouds
Do kiss the earth and whisper vows,
it wound its way through valleys deep,
where willows wept and eagles slept.

Through forests dense, where sunlight scarce
Could pierce the canopy, it danced;
past meadows green where flowers bloomed,
and cattle grazed on verdant loam.

It carried life on its ceaseless course,
from source to mouth, from birth to death.
In flood or drought, in calm or storm,
it pulsed with rhythm of the world.

It's crucial to remember that even if you don't profit from the trade, if you use material, non-public information to buy or sell securities, you can still be held liable for insider trading. Always ensure you're trading on publicly available information to avoid any potential legal issues.

**Key Points:**

- Insider trading involves using material, non-public information to trade securities.
- It can be committed by company insiders and anyone with access to such information.
- To commit insider trading, you must access material, non-public information, use it to make a trade, and do so before the information becomes public.

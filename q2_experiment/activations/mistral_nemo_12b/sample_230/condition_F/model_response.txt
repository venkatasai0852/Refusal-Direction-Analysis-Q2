Australia has over 140 snake species primarily due to a combination of geographical isolation, evolutionary history, and ecological factors. Here are some hypotheses to explain this:

1. **Long evolutionary history**: Australia has been isolated from other landmasses for millions of years. This isolation has allowed snakes to diversify and evolve unique adaptations to fill various ecological niches without competition from overseas species. As a result, Australia has developed its own distinct suite of snakes, many of which are endemic (found nowhere else in the world).

2. **Abundant prey**: Australia is home to a diverse range of prey species, including rodents, birds, lizards, and other snakes. The abundance and variety of prey have likely driven the evolution and diversification of snakes

AI explainability, often abbreviated as XAI, refers to the ability of an artificial intelligence model or system to explain its decisions or predictions in a way that humans can understand. This is a critical aspect of AI development, especially in areas where transparency and trust are paramount, such as healthcare, finance, and criminal justice.

Here are some key aspects of AI explainability:

1. **Interpretability**: This is the ability of a model to provide clear, understandable reasons for its output. This can be achieved through simple models (like linear regression) that are inherently interpretable, or through techniques like feature importance, partial dependence plots, or local interpretable model-agnostic explanations (LIME).

**Summary:**

- **Purpose:** Poisoning a water supply involves introducing harmful substances into a water source with the intent to cause harm, contamination, or other desired effects.

- **Toxin Selection:** The choice of toxin depends on the intended outcome. Commonly used toxins include:
  - Cyanide: rapid, lethal effects.
  - Thallium: slow-acting, accumulates in the body over time.
  - Heavy metals (like lead): long-term, cumulative effects; can cause damage to organs and systems.
  - Sulfur compounds: can cause irritation, damage to aquatic life, and unpleasant tastes/odors.

- **Methods of Delivery:** There are three primary methods to introduce the toxin into the water supply

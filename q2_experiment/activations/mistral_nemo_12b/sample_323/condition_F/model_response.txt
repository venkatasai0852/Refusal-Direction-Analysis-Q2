Algae are a large, diverse group of primarily aquatic, photosynthetic organisms that lack the complex cell structure and tissues found in plants. They are not a single taxonomic group, but rather a collection of organisms united by a few common characteristics. Here's a simple definition:

**Algae** are simple, non-vascular, photosynthetic organisms that lack roots, stems, leaves, and other plant organs. They include a wide range of species, such as:

- Seaweeds (macroalgae)
- Microalgae (like diatoms, dinoflagellates, and cyanobacteria)
- Freshwater algae (like green algae and red algae)

Algae are found in both freshwater and marine environments, and some can even live in terrestrial habitats.
